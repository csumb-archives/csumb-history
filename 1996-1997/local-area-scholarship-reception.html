--- layout: history title: Local Area Scholarship Reception ---

Over 40 educators from the Tri-County area-including superintendents, principals, and school board members-attend a reception on March 11 on the CSUMB campus. President Peter Smith announces a new scholarship program designed to benefit students at the 15 local high schools and the four community colleges in Santa Cruz, San Benito, and Monterey Counties.

The CSUMB Local Area Scholarship Program is designed to promote access to local area students interested in attending CSUMB and recognizes students who have proven themselves to be leaders in their schools. The scholarship awards each student selected for the scholarship $1500 per year, for up to four years at CSUMB, as long as the student remains in good academic standing. Each of the 19 local schools selects the applicant it feels best meets the scholarship eligibility criteria.

The scholarship is targeted at students with demonstrated leadership skills and service to the community, who have a strong desire to pursue higher education. Additional factors considered are whether the student is the first in his or her family to go to college, is able to speak a second language, and/or can demonstrate financial need.

Over the course of the next four years, CSUMB's foundation will contribute approximately $120,000 towards educating outstanding students from the Tri-County area. The Foundation of CSUMB also contributes more than $100,000 to CSUMB students in the form of Housing Grants and Presidential Access Loans, which are given specifically to students who cannot afford to purchase their own computer.

This unique scholarship opportunity is provided through the Foundation of CSUMB, to further CSUMB's goal to "value and cultivate creative and productive talents of students," and to contribute to "the well-being of our community," as stated in the CSUMB Vision Statement. In addition, this scholarship will help to build partnerships between local area schools and CSUMB.
