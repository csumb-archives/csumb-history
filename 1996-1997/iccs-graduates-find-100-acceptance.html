--- layout: history title: ICCS Graduates Find 100% Acceptance ---

Every one of CSUMB's Institute for Community Collaborative Studies (ICCS) graduates who applies to graduate programs is accepted into the Master of Social Work (MSW) program of his or her choice.

Laura Riggs, Suzanne Garliepp, Devena Wilson, and Rachael Hendricks, May graduates from CSUMB, validate the success of the ICCS program at CSUMB by gaining acceptance into programs across the country.

Riggs, Garliepp, and Wilson begin their next level of education at San Jose State University, while Hendricks attends Penn State University. Each student enters programs with a great deal of experience as well as a bachelor's degree from CSUMB.

Riggs contributed to the Woman's Resource Network (WRN) for her Senior Capstone project at CSUMB. (WRN maintains a website that refers women to organizations that can aid them with health issues.)

Hendricks performed AIDS outreach and education at an internship at Monterey County AIDS Project (MCAP). This experience led Hendricks to create a plan for an AIDS outreach program in Puerto Rico. The plan includes sending twelve people, medical workers and volunteers, to Puerto Rico to provide education, testing, and services to communities dealing with AIDS. Hendricks created this plan for her Capstone project and will attempt to make the program a reality after graduating from Penn.

The accomplishments and future plans of these graduates reflect the strength of the Institute for Community Collaborative Studies (ICCS) program at CSUMB, which has grown since its establishment in the fall of 1996. (In 1996 there were 18 people in the major, and by Spring 1998, there will be 60.) The major's requirements and competencies were developed in conjunction with a survey of human services professionals in the region.

The ICCS has deep roots into the community. In fact, the program began after Dr. Robert Melton, who directed the Monterey County Health Department for 19 years, sent a letter to President Smith, expressing the need for this major at CSUMB.
