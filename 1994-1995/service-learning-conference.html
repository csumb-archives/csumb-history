--- layout: history title: Service Learning Conference ---

As the new president, faculty, and staff arrive on campus, several leaders in the service learning movement arrange a conference in the Music Hall to speak and facilitate small-group discussions about this piece of the CSUMB Vision.

Service learning is a form of experiential education whereby a student is involved in community service directly correlated to the academic subject being learned. The student is asked to reflect on academic and community learning, and is taught and supported to be a respectful participant in community. Students are given a unique perspective of the "outside world" while being taught to understand diversity and reciprocity. As they give service, the students also receive through their learning about the world-not just seeing things from a theoretical, traditionally academic perspective.

Helen Rucker, Bill Melendez, Tim Stanton of Stanford, and CSUMB's Marian Penn meet with the entire CSUMB community-and potential community partners-to introduce service learning as a significant piece of CSUMB life. For many faculty members, it is their first experience on campus, and the conference helps them understand from the get-go this integral part of the CSUMB experience and its importance in the community. The conference is created to begin forging alliances about teaching in this way. Attendees split up into groups and talk about definitions and feelings about service, and shared reflections on how service could fit into an academic/learning context.

NOTE: Marian Penn is credited by many with "never letting it out of everyone's mind" that service learning would be an integral part of CSUMB life. "We all thought it was so critical to be out in the community," recalls one planning faculty member.
