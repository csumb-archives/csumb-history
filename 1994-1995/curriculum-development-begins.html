      • State of the University

President Peter Smith devises a "think piece" on developmental goals and curriculum which identifies five dominant characteristics for the CSUMB curriculum. It will be: interdisciplinary, active, reflective, team-based, and learner-centered. He says, "Our communities . . . will not settle for a warmed-over version of existing . . . programs. They will hold us accountable for creating a university that is purposeful in the creation of world-class learning opportunities and outcomes for all its students . . . and which creates a new model for education in the process." Eventually, 11 degree programs and three degree options are pre-approved by the California Postsecondary Education Commission (CPEC). The Integrated Studies Special Major is the first. This program will serve as the model for the entire "interdisciplinary" curriculum. "The design of this degree came from the belief that ideas would percolate and there would be spinoff majors," says Armando Arias, who later becomes director of the program. The interdisciplinary approach would become much larger and integrated into the very fabric of the CSUMB curriculum.
