--- layout: history title: Administrators Speak about "Early Days" ---

Bonnie Brown, Director of Financial Aid, came from CSU Fresno in fall 1994. (She's been at CSUMB in that role ever since.)

"When you got here, it was almost like a religious cause. It was such an incredible Vision Statement. I got so wrapped up in it, I couldn't imagine [not being here]. I remember Steve Arvizu telling us: 'We're doing something different and if you guys never make a mistake, I'm going to be worried about you.' I remember thinking, 'Wow, I've never heard anything like that before.' And I thought, you know, maybe this could work."

Regarding Peter's Parties, the campus president's oft-held post-work, community-building event: "We were all so busy and we would leave each other voicemails. At those parties you could see people and check [items] off your list and get things done. It set a tone-over the years the directors have been a really close group, you could always work things out. It created the atmosphere that you could just talk things over, that we don't need a bunch of meetings or emails, we can just work on it together."

Alethea DeSoto, eventually the Director of Student Recruitment, came to CSUMB in fall 1994 from Cal Poly Pomona, where she worked as an outreach counselor. After ten years on the CSUMB campus, she left to become Dean of Counseling at Evergreen College in south San Jose.

"So many of us came from different areas, so all we knew was one another. We just had each other, so we just bonded. We had this incredible team. And we really thought we could do anything."
