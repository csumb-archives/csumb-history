--- layout: history title: CSUMB Hosts Intensive LanguageLearning Program ---

An immersive language program offered by the Center for Intensive Language and Cultures (CILC) takes place on campus during the summer. Five Asian languages, including Cantonese, Korean, Vietnamese, Thai, and Tagalog, are offered to approximately 50 students from around the U.S. The administrative agent and institution of record is the Monterey Institute of International Studies (MIIS). A grant from the National Security Education Program makes substantial scholarships available for students in good standing.

Established in May 1994 to provide intensive programs in language learning on California's central coast, the Center for Intensive Language and Cultures is a consortium of MIIS, the California State University, Monterey Peninsula College, and the University of California. The decommissioning of Fort Ord and the establishment of CSUMB, together with the distinctive language resources of the area, led to the implementation of this program.

"Monterey is increasingly known as the language capital of the world," says Richard Donovan, CSUMB's Interim Academic Scheduling Coordinator. "Programs such as this one not only support the national effort toward learning less commonly-taught languages but demonstrate the local academic institutions' abilities to work together toward a common goal."

The program involves 25 hours of class per week, with additional time for directed study and excursions. After-class activities are planned to complement coursework and extend language-learning opportunities. Students, facilitators, and instructors live in an immersive residential setting in the CSUMB dorm rooms on designated language floors.

According to Cari Maher, CSUMB's On-Site Coordinator for the Center for Intensive Language and Culture, "No other programs have such an intensive form of language submersion or offer them at any other level than beginner's level. CILC bridges that gap."
