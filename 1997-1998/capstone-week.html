--- layout: history title: Capstone Week ---

"Blazing Sopranos" is a combination of opera, song, and humor that delights audiences young and old alike on May 17. The project is one of many impressive Capstone projects required of graduating seniors and presented during the CSUMB Senior Capstone Festival week May 17-22.

The "Sopranos" program is created and presented as the Senior Capstone project for 1998 CSUMB graduate Muriel Stettler. Muriel, a member of the Monterey County Symphony chorus, is joined by Nancy Williams, a regular performer with the Monterey Opera Association and the San Francisco Opera Chorus, and Stephanie Waldrip, a drama consultant in the Carmel Unified School District and teacher at the Carmel Ballet Academy. This first public performance is the kickoff event for a planned tour of local schools that delivers Muriel's message that "music is an expression of the soul and can aid children in discovering so much about life and the history of the world."

Muriel's Capstone, which she arranges to have interpreted for the hearing impaired, allows her to synthesize her expansive learning as a liberal studies major, former preschool daycare operator, ongoing student of music, and dedicated instructional aide for students with disabilities.
