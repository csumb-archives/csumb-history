--- layout: history title: Teaching Credentials at CSUMB ---

CSUMB's Institute for Field-Based Teacher Education adds an additional program to its traditional teaching credential program-an advantageous service to classroom teachers working with emergency permits, recent college graduates, and future teachers. The CSUMB Teaching Internship Program (CTIP) is a two-year pathway of study allowing candidates to earn their Multiple Subject Teaching Credential while teaching in the classroom fulltime.

The need for future teachers has increased due to California's changing demographics of student population and class sizes in grades K-3. (In California alone, it is projected that 300,000 teachers will be needed by the year 2006.) As a response to the concerns of school districts in the Santa Cruz, San Benito, and Monterey County areas, the university's intern program is designed to increase the number of properly credentialed and trained teachers. CSUMB provides a valuable service to neighboring school districts in preparing and training teachers, who will begin teaching immediately. CSUMB will also train teachers to teach in restructured schools that value the individual and diversity, and acknowledge the innovative ways in which students learn.

While CSUMB is the lead sponsor for the teaching intern program, the Monterey County Office of Education receives and administers a grant from the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing. Grant funding for the program is $112,500 for the first year and $188,000 for the second year.

CTIP begins in July 1998, accepting 75 Intern Teachers for the 1998-1999 school year, with plans to expand to 125 candidates in the second year. CSUMB's Collaborative Education and Professional Studies faculty will work in conjunction with qualified county and school district personnel to teach courses on the CSUMB campus and via distance online learning.
