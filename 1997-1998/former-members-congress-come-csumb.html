--- layout: history title: Former Members of Congress Come to CSUMB ---

CSUMB is one of the hosts of the United States Association of Former Members of Congress (USAFMC) meeting from November 12-16. USAFMC is a bipartisan, nonprofit, educational, research and social organization. The ex-congressional members are on campus to see firsthand the changes that have occurred with the conversion of the former Fort Ord to a CSU campus. Several events allow the former respresentatives to interact with university, local high school, and community college students.

The USAFMC, founded in 1970, works to promote the public's awareness of the Congress as a democratic institution that acts both domestically and globally. The Association is also dedicated to community education. One of the curricula developed to serve the public is the "Congress to Campus Program," an outreach program initiated in 1976 to introduce secondary school and college students to political life. Participating Association members regularly visit communities throughout the country to teach, speak at local civic clubs, appear at public meetings, and give radio, television, and newspaper interviews. Among the week's events:

  • A Welcome Breakfast on November 13 at the CSUMB Meeting House. The breakfast includes presentations about CSUMB's academic curriculum and service learning.
  • As part of their university experience, the former members of Congress take part in various classes on campus on November 13. Members of the media are invited to Leon Panetta's political science class as USAFMC members observe the class.
  • On November 14, the ex-congressional members split up and travel to several different high schools and community colleges, accompanied by CSUMB student representatives.

"Most college students don't get this kind of opportunity," says senior Social and Behavioral Science major Edward Reckers. "The chance to talk with individuals who have been involved in making current history and policy decisions is the kind of real-life, experiential learning that attracted me to CSUMB."

Among the 21 former congressional members attending the conference are Bob Packwood, Anthony Beilenson, George Hochbrueckner, James Dunn, and Barbara Vucanovich.
