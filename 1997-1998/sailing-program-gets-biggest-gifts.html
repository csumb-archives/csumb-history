CSUMB receives its largest gift from an individual donor-in terms of both dollars and sheer physical size-when the Rose Evelyn, a handcrafted 56-foot sailboat, is gifted in a ceremony held at the Monterey Yacht Club on June 18. Gus and Rose Marsh generously donate the boat for use in the CSUMB sailing program.

Gus Marsh built the Rose Evelyn behind his furniture mill in Birmingham, Alabama, acting on a dream that he carried with him for 50 years after completing his service in the Navy, and following plans acquired by a wife dedicated to making his dream come true. Ignoring naysayers who told him that handmade boats made with hard work and perspiration usually sank as soon as they hit the water, Gus persisted. After four years he named the boat in honor of his wife and successfully launched the vessel. Soon after, Gus and Rose sailed the Rose Evelyn halfway around the world to New Zealand.

The Rose Evelyn complements the university's growing fleet of 13'-14' sailboats and 20'-30' keelboats that allows the university to offer sailing classes to university students and other programs for the community.
