Sky Rappoport

Then: To get things off the ground, reach for the sky. That is what the new CSUMB Alumni Association chose to do when they named Sky Rappoport as their founding president. Getting things off the ground was nothing new to Rappoport. He started classes at CSUMB in 1995, our first academic year. He earned his degree in Management and International Entrepreneurship as a member of our first graduating class in 1997.

Now: From CSUMB, Pappoport decided to start his own business, SR Management, a company that manages small businesses, raises funds for nonprofits, and coordinates special events. He is also the Finance Manager for Pacific Repertory Theater.

Nikki Sannicolas

Then: Yes, a CSUMB degree will open doors! That is the answer to a question that had been lingering since we opened ours: Will CSUMB's academic majors, which combine traditional fields of study into broad, integrated programs, be respected elsewhere? The answer is a resounding "yes." Nikki Sannicolas is proof. A 1998 graduate in Social and Behavioral Science, Sannicolas was presented with two tempting graduate school opportunities. She turned down Harvard, opting instead for Northeastern University's offer of a full-tuition scholarship, a 20 hour-per-week teaching position, and a monthly stipend (See "CSUMB Grad" story).

Now: Sannicolas is now settled into her doctoral program in Social Anthropology on the Boston campus. She reports that her education at CSUMB prepared her "remarkably well" to interact and learn with her cohort of nine other doctoral candidates, several of whom already have master's degrees. She is continuing the interdisciplinary approach that she started at CSUMB by merging sociology, anthropology, and technology in her graduate work. She plans to focus her research on how having internet access affects the quality of life for disabled and homebound persons.

Nikki Sannicolas is just one shining example of the many CSUMB graduates who have been welcomed to graduate schools and workplaces, where they are putting their 21st century undergraduate education to use-a little ahead of the curve.

Sara McClellan

Sara McClellan, who transferred from Cabrillo College, arrived at CSUMB in 1996, making an immediate splash as the first student government external representative (later called Student President). McClellan's accomplishments and activities while at CSUMB included:

  • Student Representative
  • Involved in founding of student government
  • Summa Cum Laude with a 3.95 GPA in Liberal Studies, the undergraduate teacher education program
  • President's Award for Exemplary Student Achievement at 1998 Commencement
  • Involved in the first CSUMB "constitution"
  • CSSAB (California Student Association Board member)

Shortly after graduation, Earth Systems Science & Policy (ESSP) hired her as its Program Development Analyst, helping oversee ESSP's publications and marketing efforts. McClellan is instrumental in shaping the institute's outreach to underrepresented populations and student support services.

About the leadership qualities enhanced at CSUMB, McClellan says: "There's no traditional structure, which means I have just as much ability to go out and work toward change as the next person, no matter what their position or background or mind. You know, we're all sort of equal players in the game."
