--- layout: history title: Going Clubbing ---

Is there more to CSUMB college life than studying? While academics are a high priority, much can be learned from the other components of the campus scene, like CSUMB's extensive club program. Clubs represent the interests, ideals, and activism of a unique student population, featuring as many as 40 different student organizations and representing a wide range of students.

The varied interests run from competitive sport clubs such as baseball and fencing to participatory recreational activities such as disc golf and scuba diving. Many clubs have a cultural focus, such as the Multicultural Club, African Students United, the Association for Students with DisABILITIES, and the Pacific Asian Student Union. Other clubs, like the Electric Eels, American Sign Language Club, and the Latino Business Club, form as the result of a departmental major.

In the spring, Students Hungry 4 C.H.A.N.G.E. wins the Club of the Year award. In addition to Hunger Awareness Week, this campus and community service club held two immersion experiences at Dorothy's Kitchen, allowing students to serve and work with the homeless in Salinas. The club also hosted Lakota Sioux Medicine Man Chief Leonard Crow Dog to address racial tension, collaborated with other clubs on fundraisers for nonprofits, created decorations for the Circus of the Night, and sponsored a student on the AIDS Ride 5.
