--- layout: history title: Busy Summer for Conference & Event Services ---

In its first summer of operation, CSUMB's Office of Conference and Event Services hosts a total of 1,394 visitors in 23 different programs. Visitors utilize classrooms and computer labs, stay overnight in campus residence halls, eat in the Dining Commons, and enjoy the opportunities available through the Outdoor Recreation program.

In June, the Academic Senate for California Community Colleges sponsors the first annual Technology Institute. Staying on campus for a week, 120 community college faculty members receive training in on-line course development in the university's state-of-the-art Multimedia Learning Complex. Course offerings include everything from basic word processing to image processing and web design, authoring, and management. The visiting faculty members also explore technology-assisted learning and the fundamental changes education will experience over the next decade. The CCC Academic Senate now wants to make CSUMB its annual Technology Institute site.

Aside from making a contribution to the training of educators, this program will have benefits for transfer-student recruitment as community college teachers become familiar with the campus and its leading-edge technology.

Also in June, CSUMB hosts the first annual Pacific Advanced Placement Institute (PAPI) for high school faculty members. Designed to develop and fine-tune skills in course planning, management, material selection, testing, and student preparation for the Advanced Placement Exam, the five-day program offers instruction in 13 different subjects to 225 teachers. PAPI plans to enlarge the program to 275 participants in summer 1999.
