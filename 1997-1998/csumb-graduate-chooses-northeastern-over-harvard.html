--- layout: history title: CSUMB Graduate Chooses Northeastern over Harvard ---

After graduating from CSUMB in May, Nikki Sannicolas is presented with two tempting opportunities when she receives an acceptance letter to Harvard and a full scholarship to Northeastern University in Boston, Massachusetts. Sannicolas opts for Northeastern's offer of a full scholarship, a 20-hour-per-week teaching position, and a monthly stipend.

As a Social and Behavioral Science (SBSC) major at CSUMB, Sannicolas began pursuing the transitions in communicative behaviors brought about by computer-mediated communication. Sannicolas' interests lie in the behavior and identity of people in poverty who utilize online services, the growth of children's minds and knowledge due to global access to people of different backgrounds and cultures, and the self-identity of handicapped and homebound individuals who have access to the outside world via the internet.

"Science and technology have historically had profound impacts on our lives in relation to our health, how and where we live, and the manner in which we interact, personally and politically," Sannicolas said. "I think the internet brings a whole new dimension to this."

Sannicolas begins a sociology doctoral program in the fall of 1998 at Northeastern to continue her research and teach others about the use of online services as a powerful communication tool. Faculty member Dr. George Baldwin explains that "the independent research and project-based learning labs incorporated into the SBSC program at CSUMB allowed Nikki to design a plan of study that explored her interests, provided a platform to conduct research, and afforded the opportunity to publish her findings."
