--- layout: history title: Summer Quest 1998 ---

A number of students with a strong desire to learn give up part of their summer vacations for the opportunity to participate in Summer Quest 1998-an educational program designed for academically-talented and motivated students entering sixth through ninth grades.

Beginning on July 3 on the university campus, 180 students study technology and science for two weeks, from 8:30 A.M. to 2 P.M., Monday through Friday. Students explore the internet and learn how to use KidPix and HyperStudio computer programs. They also study ecology and geology, while participating in such activities as writing, Reader's Theatre, scavenger hunts, and relay races.

This unique program is presented through a partnership between CSUMB, Monterey Peninsula College, and the local school districts. Summer Quest was founded to meet the needs of students who demonstrate an eagerness to learn.

According to Roger Dahl, program coordinator and principal at Pacific Grove's Forest Grove School and David Avenue School, "The summer program provides students with a challenging academic environment. Rather than study textbooks, students dabble in interactive games and hands-on experiments."

Science studies, which focused on the ocean last summer, focuses on the land this year. Students study the geographical history of the Monterey Peninsula, global trends, history of the species, earthquake patterns, habitat and dune restoration, owl pellet dissection, dams, condor release, endangered species, and edible plants.
