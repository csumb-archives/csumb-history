--- layout: history title: President Smith Responds to Racism Concerns ---

An anonymous email with racist content spurs members of the CSUMB community to express a rising general concern about on-campus racism at a campus rally attended by approximately 200 students, staff, faculty, and community members on March 9.

In response to the initial email, Peter Smith sends a letter via the CSUMB email system to the campus community on March 1. Some of the excerpts: "I . . . share my anger and contempt for any racist words or behavior, especially the email that was sent anonymously . . . I want to thank and congratulate the student leadership who planned and held the rally. They held the CSUMB banner high and 'walked the talk' of our Vision Statement when they called for unity of all groups against racism in our campus community and beyond. This week, I have met with students, faculty, administrators, and staff to move promptly for action. For now, let me offer an initial proposal on how we should proceed. These are specific steps (see below) we can take together now [toward] the dream in our Vision Statement that this will be a place where . . . 'all teach and learn in a climate of mutual respect and tolerance.'"

Proposed Steps:

  • Creating a Task Force on Unity and Diversity
  • Sending Task Force recommendations for a CSUMB Unity/Diversity Plan to the Vice Presidents and the President
  • The convening of a "day of listening" where members of the campus community are invited to share ideas and concerns as well as successes and possible actions that will lead to the campus climate we all seek
  • The goal is to create an ongoing process and structure at CSUMB that supports a climate that respects all people, a climate for success for all campus community members
