--- layout: history title: CSU Approves CSUMB's Master Plan ---

The California State University Board of Trustees votes to approve CSUMB's Master Plan and certifies its final Environmental Impact Report. The vote, which is unanimous, takes place May 12 during the regularly-scheduled Board of Trustees meeting held at the CSU headquarters in Long Beach.

The approval of the Master Plan allows the university to continue its growth and ultimately sustain a campus with approximately 25,000 full-time equivalent students at full build-out by the year 2030. The campus is expected to reach 50% build-out, with 12,500 full-time equivalent students, by the year 2015.

CSUMB is committed to developing non-traditional methods of providing classes. Two ways the university anticipates fulfilling its long-term enrollment expectations are through distance learning programs-which allow students to enroll in the university and attend classes via computer-and short-term intensive classes. Innovative programs such as these allow CSUMB to reach students who, because of work, family, or job-related pressures, are normally unable to attend university classes on campus. In addition to being more convenient for many traditionally underrepresented students, distance and intensive learning programs also help lessen the physical impact of the expected student population.
