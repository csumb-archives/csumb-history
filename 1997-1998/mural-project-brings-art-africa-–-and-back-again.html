--- layout: history title: Mural Project Brings Art to Africa – and Back Again ---

Victoria Shaw is a professional photographer in Seattle; her mission in life is to develop something that kids all over the world can share and enjoy together. The result is the Mural Project, a traveling piece of art drawn solely for and by kids.

Kim Woods is a CSUMB student and member of the board of directors of The Mural Project, who volunteers as part of her studies in art and science. Woods became involved with the Mural Project because of CSUMB's requirements that each student participate in a service-learning class. Woods decided to do her service in Tanzania, East Africa, where she taught English to children. She met Shaw, the project director, in Arusha-the town in which she had lived while teaching the previous summer. Shaw was doing her second mural with local school children, and Woods decided to help with the mural in Tanzania.

Once the paper is laid out and the crayons dispensed, the kids take over-the only directions are to draw their favorite animal. Thereafter, pieces of the mural travel with Shaw and her volunteers from one destination to the next. One piece of a mural done by kids in Tanzania is shown to kids in Seattle. There are three installations of the mural completed by 1997. The first is at Woodland Park Zoo in Seattle, the second in Arusha, Tanzania, and the latest in Chimicum, Washington.

"What's amazing to me," explains Woods, "was how each culture affected the individual drawings. The kids in Chimicum had all these beautiful cheetahs, elephants, lions, giraffes, and monkeys while my students in Tanzania drew dogs and rats, cows and hens. It was startling to realize that the kids who lived literally next door to the African savanna had never seen it."

The mural eventually travels back to Africa, with destinations including Rwanda, Namibia, and Zambia. Later travels include India, Europe, and South America.

Once back and settled into CSUMB campus life, Woods continues working in fields that pertain to her integrated studies of science and public art. She feels that being on the board of directors for the Mural Project and working with Victoria Shaw is one way to further her studies.

"I learned so much in helping with this project. It combines the major things I care about, children, animals, and art, and enables me to share what I love with others," says Woods.
