The first Leon Panetta Lecture Series, entitled "Governing our Democracy into the 21st Century - Soundbites or Solutions," includes four lectures focusing on the branches of our federal government. The series is presented by the Leon & Sylvia Panetta Institute for Public Policy.

In the first event, Tim Russert, host of NBC's Meet the Press, moderates the lively discussion between Mr. Panetta and James Baker III, former Chief of Staff to Presidents Ronald Reagan and George H.W. Bush, on the challenges of the presidency. Approximately 500 students and community members attend the lecture on October 20, where tickets are sold on a first-come, first-served basis to members of the community. The lecture takes less than an hour to sell out. Students from local high schools, community colleges, UCSC, and CSUMB attend the lecture on academic scholarships. This lecture is aired nationally on C-SPAN on December 13.

The series continues on January 19, with U.S. House Democratic Leader Richard Gephardt and U.S. House Budget Committee Chairman John Kasich. Moderated by Bob Schieffer of CBS's Face the Nation, the event focuses on the U.S. Congress and the challenges facing the legislative branch.

The third installment of the series takes place on March 9, with Henry Cisneros and Jack Kemp, both former Secretaries of the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development. Cokie Roberts, Co-anchor of This Week and ABC Special Correspondent, moderates the discussion about the Cabinet.

The final Leon Panetta Lecture, on May 4, focuses on the Supreme Court. Leon Panetta moderates the discussion between Supreme Court Justice Antonin Scalia and Former White House Counsel Abner Mikva.
