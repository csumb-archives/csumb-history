--- layout: history title: Hunger Awareness Week ---

University students, staff, and faculty host the first annual Hunger Awareness Week in November. "Students Hungry 4 Change," a non-profit organization at CSUMB made up of students whose aim is to address the issue of hunger globally and in the Monterey area, co-sponsors (with CSUMB) a series of events intended to raise awareness about the issues of hunger and homelessness in the U.S.

The students choose this week to segue into International Hunger and Homelessness Awareness Week. The week's events are held on campus and include expert speakers from the community, student lectures, and multi-media presentations. On November 5, Patrick Giantonio presents "Footsteps into Change," a multi-media presentation documenting his walk across Africa that raised awareness about hunger. November 6 features a hunger banquet, which serves to acknowledge the unequal distribution of food around the world. All events are free but participants contribute canned goods at each event.

Throughout the week, barrels remain in the central quad for those not attending the week's events but who wish to donate food. The student-organized events are the result of hard work and a commitment to realizing CSUMB's vision "to integrate work and learning, service and reflection, in order to produce productive citizens with social responsibility and skills to be community builders."
