--- layout: history title: Celebration of Community ---

The inaugural CSUMB Celebration of Community is held on May 21. The event welcomes citizens from surrounding communities to the university campus as it names CSUMB's first class of Fellows-the highest nonacademic recognition the university can give for service to the community and to the university. In addition to honoring 11 important individuals, the inaugural CSUMB Celebration of Community also raises money to support local scholarship and educational programs.

The first class of 11 Fellows are local and state leaders who have dedicated themselves to building community in California and who have made CSUMB's strong early development a priority. These honorees represent several important elements of life in the Monterey region:

  • In agribusiness, Bob Antle has been a pioneer in establishing lettuce as a primary crop
  • In business, Ted Balestreri is a national leader in the American hospitality industry
  • In community and human services, Jim Gattis has been the innovator and driving force behind a number of events that have raised millions of dollars for charity
  • In community and human services, Ron Johnson has been a positive role model for youth through his career in professional football and active involvement with the Boys and Girls Club of the Monterey Peninsula
  • In community and human services, CSUMB's own Marian Penn has been one of the nation's pioneers in infusing community service into academic programs
  • In education, Dr. Charles Reed, the Chancellor of the CSU system, is a national authority on higher education
  • In the military, Lieutenant General James E. Moore Jr., U.S. Army (Retired) has been actively involved with the redevelopment planning of Fort Ord
  • In public service, Leon Panetta has dedicated more than 30 years as a leader representing both the local region and the country
  • In sports and wellness, Peter Cutino posted the best all-time record as a water polo coach in national collegiate competition
  • In technology, Brenda Laurel has been a computer games pioneer whose commitment to gender equity led to innovative changes in entertainment for girls
