--- layout: history title: Grant Forges Community Arts Partnership ---

CSUMB initiates RUAP&#8212the Reciprocal University for the Arts Project&#8212after being selected as one of only six sites nationally by the Lila Wallace-Reader's Digest Fund to receive a grant to finance an arts partnership with surrounding communities.

Driven by CSUMB's visionary team of Dr. Amalia Mesa-Bains, Director of the Visual and Public Arts Institute, and Richard Bains, Director of the Music and Performing Arts Institute, the program benefits from multiple community partnerships that enhance the value of the four-year $600,000 grant. Goals for the project include building youth self-esteem and resiliency through the arts, increasing student enrollment at CSUMB and other colleges, expanding CSUMB student learning about community, developing supportive relationships among art and youth-serving organizations, and using arts programming to change the regional perception of local communities.

"Through the arts we will foster cultural citizenship, community pride, and recognition of diverse communities' funds of knowledge," explains Dr. Mesa-Bains.

NOTE: The "visionary team" of Bains and Mesa-Bains are a team at home, as well&#8212they're married.
