--- layout: history title: Distributed Learning Starts in Fall ---

Late in the 19th century, schools began offering the first correspondence courses, the earliest form of distance education or "distributed learning." Starting in the fall of 2000, CSUMB will offer its first distributed learning program-the Distributed Liberal Studies Degree Completion Program (DLSDCP).

The curriculum will be designed for working students who have completed junior college, want a Bachelors degree in Liberal Studies, and are interested in a teaching career. According to Dan Granger, CSUMB's Director of Distributed Learning & Extended Education, "distributed learning has historically been learner-centered and has had increased popularity worldwide in the last 30 years.

"The notion of distributed learning has been to rethink effective teaching and learning in order to serve students who can't get access to learning in a conventional classroom format and," Granger says, "this is why it is a perfect fit with CSUMB's Vision. Once students receives their bachelor's degree through CSUMB's distributed learning program, it will enable them to go right into teaching with the CalState Teach program."

CalState Teach is another distributed learning program that helps students who have an "emergency teaching credential" begin teaching while they work toward obtaining their official teaching certificate.
