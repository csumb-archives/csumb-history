--- layout: history title: Radio @ Lightspeed ---

On October 29, CSUMB begins streaming a live radio show through cyberspace. Produced by students from the Community Radio class taught by Steven Levinson of the Teledramatic Arts and Technology Institute, Bored on the Ord delivers music and commentary via the internet.

The relatively new "streaming" technology allows anyone in the world with a computer, Web connection, and free RealPlayer® software to pick up the show. (Feedback from a listener in Japan indicates the show is reaching an outlying audience.) Initially airing just two hours per day, the online radio show eventually pipes music around the clock&#8212with live hosts from noon until 2 P.M.--Monday through Friday.

This collaborative effort by TAT, Student Voice, the Work Group, and New Media Services utilizes CSUMB streaming capabilities developed by Ski Romagnoli and Hossien Koch. Bored on the Ord expands CSUMB's on-air presence that has also included segments aired locally on community radio station KAZU (90.3 FM).
