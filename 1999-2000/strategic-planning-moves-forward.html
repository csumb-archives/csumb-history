--- layout: history title: Strategic Planning Moves Forward ---

A series of strategic "visioning" sessions at the University Center in November enjoy a combined attendance of over 100 students, staff, faculty, and administrators. Participants brainstorm on how to transfer the university's Vision Statement into a strategic plan.

Over a one-month period, CSUMB Interim Provost Diane Cordero de Noriega&#8212along with Joe Larkin, the Assistant Vice President for Academic Development, and Sara McClellan, the strategic planning process facilitator&#8212host five strategic planning sessions.

"Everyone says that we have a really broad Vision Statement and everyone has their own interpretations of the Vision," the provost explains. "I agree with that, but I don't think that's necessarily a bad thing. It's only a bad thing if we don't have a clear direction."

With the release of the Western Association of Schools and Colleges (WASC) external team's report on the overall direction of CSUMB, Cordero de Noriega says there is a heightened sense of urgency to develop and implement a strategic plan. According to the provost, the report made it very clear that the university needed to develop a plan right away.

The strategic planning team names an expanded strategic planning committee, which is selected based on nominations from the Student Voice, Senate Executive Committee, and the university's Centers. The committee is made up of students, staff, faculty, and community members. Beginning with a December 14 dinner meeting, the committee will start work on the creation of a "rolling" action plan in order to develop a multi-year strategy.
