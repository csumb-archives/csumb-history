President Smith charges Beverly Grova, Senior Director of Development, with formation of the President's Council. Meeting for the first time in September, an impressive group of community leaders join campus representatives to guide CSUMB's private fundraising efforts. Mark Johnson, an estate-planning attorney, chairs the 39-member President's Council, the mission of which is to identify and provide private funding resources to CSUMB in support of program, facility, and equipment priorities identified by campus leadership.

The council forms five committees guided by esteemed leaders: Bob Antle, CEO of Tanimura and Antle, chairs the Facilities Projects committee; Peggy Downes-Baskin, professor and author, oversees the Academic Excellence committee; Mary Kay Higgins, a vice president at Merrill Lynch, heads the Planned Giving committee; community leader Kevin Cartwright directs the Community Connections committee; and Jaime Rosso, CEO of Rosso Furniture, chairs the Student Success committee.
