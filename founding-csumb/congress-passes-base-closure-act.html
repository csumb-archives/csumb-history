--- layout: history title: Congress Passes Base Closure Act ---

The Defense Base Closure and Realignment Act of 1990, passed by the U.S. Congress, is signed into law by President George Herbert Walker Bush on November 5, 1990. This legislation puts the base closure process on a formal track.

The legislation creates the Department of Defense Base Realignment and Closure Commission (BRAC). BRAC is charged with selecting bases for closure nationwide, and with creating a plan for the realignment of military resources. The commission is required to deliver its recommendations for base closures in eight months-by July 1, 1991. Communities around the United States prepare for the unthinkable as they face the abrupt loss of a major force in their local economies, and a whole segment of their population.
