--- layout: history title: The Idea in Writing ---

The possibility of a stand-alone CSU campus at Fort Ord is first formally broached in a letter from David Leveille, Institutional Director of the California State University system, to Warren H. Fox, Executive Director of the California Postsecondary Education Commission (CPEC). Leveille's letter of September 30, 1991 states: "Among the ideas put forth by San Jose State University and [CSU] Chancellor's Office personnel are the relocation of the Monterey County off-campus center of San Jose State University (currently located in Salinas in leased facilities, serving approximately 500 students) and possible future expansion into a full-service campus to serve the region."

In addition to local officials, discussions are moving forward with state and federal officials to convey the ideas put forward. The concepts under discussion include 700-1,000 acres of the 28,000-acre base for future use by the university.
