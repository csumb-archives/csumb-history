--- layout: history title: An OffCampus Center of San Jose State? ---

Several representatives of San Jose State University (SJSU) meet with Congressman Leon Panetta on July 3 in Santa Cruz to explore the possibility of moving San Jose State's Salinas off-campus center to Fort Ord. (Some report that it was California State Senator Henry Mello who first approached San Jose State to suggest this idea.) At this point, over 500 San Jose State students are enrolled at its Salinas off-campus center, and San Jose State's lease will expire in about four years. San Jose State is interested in 700 acres of the base, a site it considers sufficient to accommodate 15,000 students.

San Jose State University officials attending the meeting include: outgoing President Gail Fullerton, Interim President Handel Evans, Academic Vice President Arlene Okerland, and Director of Operations, Design and Construction Richard Hendrickson. Congressman Panetta responds favorably to their proposal.
