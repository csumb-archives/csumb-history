Legislation parented by California State Senator Henry Mello (D-Watsonville) creates the Fort Ord Reuse Authority (FORA), a regional governing body made up of Monterey County and the eight cities within the county. The bill passes on
May 9.

FORA is organized as a public corporation of the State of California, whose authority stems from California Government Code Section 67655-67700. FORA will be governed by a board which includes representatives of Monterey County and the cities of Monterey, Marina, Seaside, Del Rey Oaks, Carmel, Sand City, Pacific Grove, and Salinas.

FORA's chartering legislation states that: "The board shall prepare, adopt, review, revise from time to time, and maintain a plan for the future use and development of the territory occupied by Fort Ord as of January 1, 1993. The adopted plan shall be the official local plan for the reuse of the base for all public purposes, including all discussions with the Army and other federal agencies, and for purposes of planning, design, and funding by all state agencies." The legislation states that FORA may, at its discretion, begin with the plan created by the Fort Ord Reuse Group (FORG).

FORA is charged with the creation of a plan for the integrated management of: land use, including the creation of criteria and standards for the uses of land, water, air, space, and other natural resources within the area of the base; the development of a system of roadways, transit facilities, air transportation facilities, terminals and other facilities for the movement of people and goods to, from, and within the area of the base; the preservation, development, use, and management of natural resources within the area of the base; the development, use, and management of the recreational resources within the area of the base; and a capital improvement program, including allocation of the available water supply, sewage treatment and disposal capacity, and other public service capabilities.

On May 20, the FORA board is sworn into office, for the first time creating a formal authority for the reuse of the Fort Ord site.
