--- layout: history title: News of the Times ---
  • January 1, 1993 - Czechoslovakia divides, creating the independent nations Slovakia and Czech Republic.

  • January 3 - In Moscow, George H. W. Bush and Boris Yeltsin sign the second Strategic Arms Reduction Treaty (START II).

  • January 6 - Ballet Dancer Rudolf Nureyev dies of AIDS, followed on February 3 by tennis legend Arthur Ashe. The number of known AIDS deaths in the U.S. this year is 41,920.

  • January 18 - For the first time, the Martin Luther King Jr. holiday is officially observed in all 50 U.S. states.

  • January 19 - IBM announces a $4.97 billion loss for 1992, the largest single-year corporate loss in United States history.

  • January 19 - Iraq refuses to allow UNSCOM inspectors to use its own aircraft to fly into Iraq, and begins military operations in the demilitarized zone between Iraq and Kuwait and the northern No-Fly Zone. U.S. forces fire approximately 40 Tomahawk cruise missiles at Baghdad factories linked to Iraq's nuclear weapons program. Iraq then informs UNSCOM that it will be able to resume its flights.

  • January 20 - William Jefferson Clinton succeeds George H. W. Bush as President of the United States of America.

  • January 26 - Václav Havel is elected President of the Czech Republic.

  • February 26 - In New York City, a van bomb parked below the North Tower of the World Trade Center explodes, killing six and injuring over a thousand.

  • February 28 - Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco and Firearms agents raid the Branch Davidian compound in Waco, Texas with a warrant to arrest cult leader David Koresh on federal firearms violations. Four agents and five Davidians die in the raid and a 51-day standoff begins.

  • March 4 - Authorities announce the capture of suspected World Trade Center bombing conspirator Mohammad Salameh.

  • March 9 - Rodney King testifies at the federal trial of four Los Angeles, California police officers accused of violating King's civil rights when they beat him during an arrest.

  • March 11 - Janet Reno is confirmed by the United States Senate and sworn in the next day, becoming the first female Attorney General of the United States.

  • March 12 - Several bombs explode in Bombay, India, killing about 300 and injuring hundreds more.

  • March 12 - North Korea says that it plans to withdraw from the Nuclear Nonproliferation Treaty and refuses to allow inspectors access to nuclear sites.

  • March 22 - The Intel Corporation ships the first Pentium chips.

  • March 27 - Jiang Zemin becomes President of the People's Republic of China.

  • March 31 - A bug in a program written by Richard Depew sends an article to 200 newsgroups simultaneously. The term "spamming" is coined by Joel Furr to describe the incident.

  • April - The Kuwaiti government claims to uncover an Iraqi assassination plot against former U.S. President George H. W. Bush shortly after his visit to Kuwait. Two Iraqi nationals, caught with smuggled hashish and alcohol inside Kuwait, confess to driving a car-bomb into Kuwait on behalf of the Iraqi Secret Service.

  • April 6 - A nuclear accident occurs at Russia's Tomsk 7 nuclear reprocessing plant in Siberia, contaminating almost 120 square kilometers of the surrounding province of Tomsk. The explosion occurs during plutonium-separation activities at the plant, which covers an area the size of Paris. For the first time in the accident-plagued history of the Soviet/Russian nuclear program, the Russian government notifies the public of the incident.

  • April 10 - ANC activist Chris Hani is assassinated in South Africa.

  • April 19 - The 51-day siege of the Branch Davidian compound in Waco, Texas ends when the FBI initiates a tear gas attack, shots are fired, and the compound is completely consumed in fire. Between 72 and 86 men, women, and children die in the fire, including cult leader David Koresh.

  • April 22 - Version 1.0 of the Mosaic web browser is released.

  • April 23 - The World Health Organization declares tuberculosis a Global Emergency.

  • May 1 - A suicide bomber assassinates President Premadasa of Sri Lanka.

  • May 24 - Eritrea declares independence from Ethiopia, two years after Eritrean forces defeated the Ethiopian army, and thirty years after Eritrea's annexation by Ethiopia touched off a civil war.

  • June 6 - Mongolia holds its first direct presidential elections.

  • June 27 - U.S. President Bill Clinton orders a cruise missile attack on Iraqi intelligence headquarters in the Al-Mansur District of Baghdad, in response to the attempted assassination of former U.S. President George H. W. Bush during his visit to Kuwait in mid-April.

  • July 12 - A magnitude 7.8 earthquake off Hokkaido, Japan launches a devastating tsunami, killing 202 on the small island of Okushiri.

  • August 4 - A federal judge sentences LAPD officers Stacey Koon and Laurence Powell to 30 months in prison for violating the rights of motorist Rodney King.

  • August 21 - NASA loses radio contact with the Mars Observer orbiter three days before the spacecraft is scheduled to enter orbit around Mars.

  • September 13 - Palestine Liberation Organization (PLO) leader Yasir Arafat and Israeli prime minister Yitzhak Rabin shake hands in Washington, D.C., after signing a peace accord.

  • September 29 - An earthquake centred on Killari, Maharashtra, India kills nearly 10,000 people.

  • On October 3 - In Mogadishu, Somalia, a 17-hour urban battle ends with 18 U.S. soldiers dead and eighty-four wounded. Hundreds of Somalis also die. International news stations show bodies of dead American soldiers being dragged through the streets of Mogadishu.

  • October 7 - President Clinton announces the withdrawal of U.S. troops from Somalia.

  • November 1 - The Maastricht Treaty activates, formally establishing the European Union.

  • November 9 - The 16th-century Stari Most bridge of Mostar, Bosnia and Herzegovina, one of the most enduring symbols of Bosnian unity, is destroyed by tank fire.

  • November 18 - In South Africa, 21 political parties approve a new constitution.

  • November 20 - The U.S. Senate Ethics Committee issues a stern censure of California Senator Alan Cranston for his "dealings" with savings-and-loan executive Charles Keating in the savings and loan scandal.

  • November 30 - U.S. President Bill Clinton signs the Brady Handgun Violence Prevention Act (the Brady Bill) into law.

  • December 2 - NASA launches the Space Shuttle Endeavour on a mission to repair an optical flaw in the Hubble Space Telescope.

  • December 2 - Colombian drug lord Pablo Escobar is gunned down in Medellín when police try to arrest him.

  • December 12, 1993 - A major earthquake hits Flores, Indonesia, killing 2200 people.
