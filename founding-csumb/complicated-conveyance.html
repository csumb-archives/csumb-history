--- layout: history title: A Complicated Conveyance ---

Hank Hendrickson, Executive Dean of CSUMB, records this in his timeline of events in April 1994:

"Pursuant to Public Law 103-160, section 2903 (also known as the Pryor Amendment), we submitted a letter of request to the Secretary of the Army, The Honorable Togo West, Jr., for direct Economic Development Conveyance (EDC) of approximately 1,350 acres of Fort Ord property at no cost and without restrictions on use by the university. For many reasons (mainly less restrictions on our use of the land) this was the preferred method of conveyance, as opposed to the Public Benefit Conveyance (PBC) route through the Department of Education [DOE].

"Our submission of the EDC application without prior notification and discussions with the DOE did not sit well with this federal department. Because of our asking for an EDC conveyance in lieu of a PBC, our relationship with the Department of Education cooled considerably."
