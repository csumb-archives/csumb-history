--- layout: history title: Grant Helps Local Watershed Management and More ---

In July, research conducted by Earth Systems Science & Policy professors (Drs. Lars Pierce, John Stamm, Jack Paris, and Rikk Kvitek) and seven CSUMB students gets a boost, thanks to a grant jointly funded by NASA and the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). The $700,000 grant funds the creation of a computer-based tool that gives local communities large-scale estimates of the effects of various land management strategies&#8212for example, urban expansion, agricultural practices, or restoration projects&#8212on regional water resources.

The three-dimensional picture technology is created with a geographic information system (GIS) to overlay a LANDSAT satellite image onto a digital topographic map. (The perspective is similar to what one might see when flying about 3000 feet above CSUMB and looking due east toward Salinas and Fremont Peak.) By applying this incredible technology to create projections, Dr. Pierce, his colleagues, and his students enable local areas&#8212and other communities across the nation&#8212to adopt the best possible policies for watershed management.
