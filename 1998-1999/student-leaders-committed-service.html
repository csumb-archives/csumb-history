--- layout: history title: Student Leaders Committed to Service ---

CSUMB's Service Learning involves students in meaningful service to surrounding communities. To develop student leadership, the CSUMB Service Learning Institute sponsors the Summer of Service Leadership Academy (SoSLA), an intensive four-week training program for a dozen students each summer. SoSLA's goal is to help participants develop as responsible citizens who realize and act upon their leadership potential for the good of the community. The training provides students with the tools for service leadership, including facilitation skills, awareness of strengths and passions, and understanding of how to create and implement social action projects.

As of July 1998, over 35 students have participated in SoSLA. Throughout the academic year, these committed leaders put their training to work as University Service Advocates (USAs) who support service learning faculty, foster community partnerships, and strengthen the ethic of service on campus. USAs serve as teaching assistants for Service Learning courses, work to strengthen specific Service Learning connections, and work directly with selected community programs such as the Boys and Girls Club of Seaside, the Volunteer Center of Monterey County, and Alisal Healthy Start. In addition, USAs work to involve the campus community in social action projects such as Hunger Awareness Week.
