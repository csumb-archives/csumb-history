--- layout: history title: CSUMB Professor's Idea Gets GoAhead ---

CSUMB Professor Geri Philley receives a grant from America Online (AOL) to work with Georgia Grijalva, principal of Juan Cabrillo Elementary School in Seaside, in the conceptualization of "The Virtual Outreach Project." The concept is to keep parents informed about their children's education and to promote communication between parents and their children's schools.

Geri's Spring 1998 Multimedia 2 class created a rapid prototype of the electronic kiosk that she and Grijalva envisioned; it provides a means of disseminating information among parents and encourages them to participate in their children's education. It offers an easy way for parents to provide the school with basic information, such as reporting absences and changes of address. The advantage of the kiosk, which eventually goes online, is that it provides parents access to information regardless of their reading, writing, and language proficiencies. Another component of the kiosk is aimed at instructing parents on how to help their children succeed in school and emphasizing the importance of higher education. The funding also provides for bilingual tutoring for Cabrillo students in the use of computers. These students will in turn help their parents use the kiosk and online services.
