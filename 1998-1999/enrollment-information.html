      • State of the University

In addition to returning students, approximately 800 new students are registered (319 are first-time freshmen), bringing enrollment for the start of classes to 1,958. Students choose from a menu of approximately 540 courses, 25% more courses than in Fall 1997. Total enrollment at CSUMB reaches approximately 1,900 full-time-equivalent (FTE) students this semester, an increase from 1,530 FTE students in spring 1998. Approximately 650 students were enrolled when CSUMB first opened in August 1995.

This year's increase is due to a growing awareness of CSUMB and its unique "outcomes-based" education. According to Alethea DeSoto, CSUMB's Director of Student Outreach and Recruitment, "[CSUMB's] . . . outreach casts a wide net to embrace a multitude of diverse peoples while striving to meet the needs of individuals. Students are continually attracted to CSUMB because of its principles of outcomes-based education, where it's not which classes you take that's important but what you learn and know that matters."

For the first time in recent years, the cost of attending college has actually decreased, which is being attributed to the improved economic position of California.

The majority of both new and returning students will live on campus. Freshmen and sophomores are encouraged to live in the residence halls and students with families and upper-division students are eligible to live in on-campus apartments. Seven full-time faculty members will join the existing 77 faculty members. In addition, more than 100 part-time faculty members have been hired to teach in the fall semester. CSUMB offers 12 undergraduate majors and three graduate programs.
