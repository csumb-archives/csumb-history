--- layout: history title: Grants to Support Wireless Projects ---

CSUMB's Wireless Education & Technology Center (WeTEC) receives two new grants of over $240,000, in addition to an earlier $236,000 grant from Hewlett-Packard. The funds will be used to deploy wireless technology across the campus and to implement a website focusing on HP Philanthropy's Mobile Education Projects, both in the U.S. and internationally.

One of the continuation grants supports current and new projects, including $122,000 in equipment and supplies and $8,000 to hire students to assist staff and faculty.

"This new grant capitalizes on the momentum our mobile education projects have achieved," says WeTEC Director Arlene Krebs. "The new projects will involve approximately 800 students and 11 faculty. Combined with our other HP projects, about 25 percent of our student body will be involved in mobile education studies."

Funds provided by the continuation grant will support:

  • Live iPAQ GIS Seafloor Mapping ~ Dr. Rikk Kvitek, Professor, SMART
  • HB Tablets: The Field Books of the 21 Century Geologist ~ Dr. Dough Smith, Professor, SMART
  • Calculations in Context: Precalculus & Calculus Mathematics ~ Dr. Don Pierce, Assistant Professor, SMART
  • HP iPAQs Physics Classes ~ Dr. Dan Fernandez, Associate Professor, SMART
  • The Virtual Language Lab: Expanding Courses & Technology with Tablets & iPAQs ~ Dr. Yoshito Saito-Abbott, Professor, AHSS; Dr. John Ittelson, Professor, SMART; Gus Leonard, Language Lab Coordinator, AHSS
