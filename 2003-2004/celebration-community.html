--- layout: history title: Celebration of Community ---

On April 28, CSUMB's black-tie dinner event, the Seventh Annual Celebration of Community, is held at the University Center. Paradise Catering provides the spectacular cuisine, but the highlight of the evening is the introduction of the people who have made significant contributions to their professions and their communities.

This year's group of Fellows join members of the community-and honorees from years past-in this much-anticipated annual affair. The event's proceeds benefit CSUMB's Local Area Scholarship Opportunity Program, which provides scholarships to students at each public high school and community college in the Tri-County area. This year's honorees are:

  • Ed Boutonnet (Agribusiness) is President and CEO of Ocean Mist Farms, and has been recognized with the Award of Excellence for Generosity and Continued Commitment to the California Agricultural Leadership Program.
  • Susan Matcham, J.D. (Business) is a partner in the law offices of Caballero, Matcham, and McCarthy.
  • Brian Contreras (Community & Public Service) is the founder and Executive Director of Second Chance Family & Youth Services, a non-profit agency that seeks to stop gang violence in the community. He has been presented with the California Peace Prize Award for his work in the field of violence prevention.
  • Margaret Bates (Education) is President of the CSUMB President's Council and was chosen Outstanding Woman for 1998 by the Monterey County Commission on the Status of Women.
  • Dr. Steven Packer (Health) is President and CEO of Community Hospital of the Monterey Peninsula.
  • John Wilmot (Military) ~ Prior to his retirement from the U.S. Army as a sergeant first class, John Wilmot was Laboratory Director for Thor Electronics Manufacturing Corporation. In 2002, he was recognized as Citizen of the Year by the City of Marina.
  • Since 1977, Dr. David Epel (Science & Technology) has served as the Jane and Marshall Steel Jr. Professor of Marine Sciences and Associate Director at the Hopkins Marine Station of Stanford University.
  • Anthony Toney (Sports) is the Unit Director for the Boys & Girls Club of Monterey County in Seaside; he was also a pro football player with the Philadelphia Eagles for five years.
  • Luis Valdez (Arts) founded El Teatro Campesino in 1965 and is its Artistic Director. He was also the founding Director of CSUMB's Teledramatic Arts and Technology department in 1995.

NOTE: Luis Valdez is CSUMB's most famous faculty member and an integral part of the university's founding group. Valdez, an inspiring figure and public speaker, is credited by many faculty members as having been the focal point for community-building among faculty member in the university's earliest days. He's a playwright, activist, screenwriter, filmmaker, and college professor (currently on indefinite leave). Valdez gathered striking farmworkers in the 1960s; founded the nationally-known El Teatro Campesino (started on a flatbed truck that went into the fields to perform for workers); wrote the hit play Zoot Suit (which made it to Broadway before becoming a motion picture starring Edward James Olmos in 1978); and wrote and directed the hit movie La Bamba (1987). By joining CSUMB in 1995, Valdez accepted his first teaching position in almost 20 years.
