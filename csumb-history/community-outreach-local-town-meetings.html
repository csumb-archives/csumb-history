--- layout: history title: Community Outreach & Local Town Meetings ---

The CSUMB President's Community Outreach Program stems from the knowledge that, over the course of America's history, many great institutions have been shaped through small town meetings and community gatherings. The Community Outreach Program develops through input from the President's Community Advisory Council, a group of 23 dedicated Tri-County community leaders who come together to strengthen CSUMB's relationship with the diverse people the university serves.

In a remarkable inaugural year, 21 CSUMB Town Meetings are thoughtfully hosted in private homes throughout Monterey, San Benito, and Santa Cruz counties. The dynamic evening gatherings give CSUMB the opportunity to provide key information about the university, while also providing community guests a forum for candid questions and comments about the university. With at least one faculty member and student accompanying President Smith to each meeting, invaluable and spirited dialogue occurs with over 360 leaders from business, professional, government, and nonprofit sectors from the surrounding communities. Through these meetings, CSUMB builds a foundation of community support and commitment that enhances its ability to provide the highest-quality education&#8212and carry forward a commitment to serve the local community.
