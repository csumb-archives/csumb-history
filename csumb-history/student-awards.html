President's Exemplary Achievement Award: Bethtina Woodridge

A Human Communication major, Bethtina Woodridge wins this award for initiating a program that fosters greater understanding of underrepresented student groups. The purpose of the President's Award for Exemplary Student Achievement is formally to recognize one outstanding graduating senior who has demonstrated exceptional contributions to CSUMB and the greater communities.

Alumni Association Vision Award: Adrian Andrade

The Alumni Association Vision Award formally recognizes one outstanding graduating senior who is the first person in his or her family to earn a college degree and who has embodied the values and goals expressed in CSUMB's Vision Statement. A Social and Behavioral Sciences major, Adrian Andrade serves as a role model for his family and community. Adrian Andrade is also recognized for his success in taking first place at the California State University Research Competition. His project explores the impact of access to education over four generations of his migrant family background. Says Andrade of his project: "The purpose . . . is to illustrate that it was primarily education that freed past generations of my family from the constraints of farm labor and thus moved the family into blue collar and professional work. Only through continuing education and attainment of higher educational degrees will current generations be able to keep the progression in action."

Scholar Athlete Award: Damion Patrick

The Scholar Athlete Award is given to Earth Systems Science & Policy major Damion Patrick for his outstanding academic performance: an average 21-credit load plus an average of 21 points per game as a member of the basketball team.

Social Justice Award: Annabelle Rodriguez

The Social Justice Award is given to Social & Behavioral Sciences major Annabelle Rodriguez by one of her community partners, Mrs. Helen Rucker. Miss Rodriguez is formally recognized because she exemplifies the social justice tenets of CSUMB's Vision Statement.

Tri-County Award: Rodolfo Puente

Another community partner and a CSUMB Fellow, Lupe Garcia, presents the Tri-County Award to Rodolfo Puente, a Liberal Studies major who coordinated a special project in service to the Tri-County community. Mr. Puente has participated actively as a positive agent of change and is a role model to other Tri-County students.

Richard Ingram, a Telecommunications Multimedia, & Applied Computing major, wins the Certificate of Recognition from the CSU Chancellor's Office.
