--- layout: history title: RISE Grant Motivates WouldBe Scientists ---

Aimed at motivating girls and children from low-income families to study science and math, the RISE (Recruitment In Science Education) project begins in fall 1999 in sixth-grade classes at Marina and Salinas schools. It's made possible due to the generosity of Roberta and Hilton Bialek and the Berkshire Foundation, who provide startup funds for the first five years of RISE. The grant, which will total $540,000 over five years, with the raising of matching funds starting in the second year, provides for a full-time counselor, an advisor from the CSUMB Earth Systems Science & Policy program, and $40,000 per year in scholarships.

RISE students will receive the scholarships after participating in the program through the 12th grade and graduating from high school. Participating students benefit from small-group instruction, tutorial assistance, field trips, science fairs, reading instruction, guidance and counseling services, intervention activities, and a weeklong summer institute held on the CSUMB campus.
