The 1999 Leon Panetta Lecture Series, The Information Age Revolution - Impact of Technology on the 21st Century, presents three events to the Monterey Bay community, and to audiences from beyond this area. The lectures include: "Media and Politics" on March 29, "Communication and Entertainment" on April 19, and "Business, Education and Society" on May 17, and the series is presented by the Leon & Sylvia Panetta Institute for Public Policy.

In the first lecture, David Broder, National Political Correspondent for the Washington Post, appears with Alan Simpson, the Director of the Institute of Politics at Harvard University, to discuss media and politics.

In the next lecture, former White House Press Secretary Mike McCurry appears with Jack Valenti, Chairman and CEO of the Motion Picture Association, to discuss communication and entertainment.

The series concludes with a discussion on business, education and society, bringing together Marc Andreesen, Netscape founder and AOL Chief Technical Officer; Dr. Joyce Brothers, psychologist, author and columnist; and Larry Irving, Assistant Secretary of the U.S. Department of Commerce National Telecommunications and Information Administration.

In addition, on November 29, 1999, the Panetta Institute holds a Social Security Forum, with the Honorable Ken Apfel, Social Security Commissioner, as the main speaker and Leon Panetta as moderator. This event is held in the University Center at CSUMB.
