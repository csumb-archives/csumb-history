--- layout: history title: International Exchange Leads to Global Awareness ---

While advanced technology and dedicated faculty allow the CSUMB community to bring global experiences directly to campus, there is no substitute for actual travel to other places on the globe. The Quéretaro Exchange Program is sponsored by the CSUMB Social and Behavioral Sciences Center's Institute for Mexican and U.S.-Mexican Studies, and involves eight to twelve CSUMB students in a summer field experience in Mexico.

The program is conducted with the collaboration of faculty and students from the University of Querétaro (UAQ), a comprehensive, major regional university with 18,000 students. Querétaro is a Colonial city of some 650,000 inhabitants, and numerous rural towns and peasant and craft-producing communities surround it. The CSUMB students live in the homes of community members in the rural towns. They receive training on the history, culture, and geography of the area from University of Querétaro faculty and each student is expected to carry out a specific field research project, which they plan before they leave. They receive field supervision from CSUMB faculty while they carry out their projects and research. Students write the results of their research as a formal report in the fall following their research experience.
