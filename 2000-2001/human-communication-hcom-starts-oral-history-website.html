--- layout: history title: Human Communication (HCOM) Starts Oral History Website ---

In December, the students and faculty of the Human Communication (HCOM) course "Oral History and Community Memory" present a website on First Generation College Students at CSUMB.

The website is based on the students' oral history interviews of first-generation college students, with a focus on freshmen, sophomores, and juniors who participated in the FOCUS Summer Bridge program* before their first semester at CSUMB. In some cases, students are interviewed both as freshmen and two years later as juniors.

According to the website, "Most high schools employ a tracking system that labels students as headed in the direction of either vocational training, a junior college, or a university after graduating. Students are then placed in courses that correspond to how they have been labeled by the guidance department. Many schools place students on a track according to their score on a standardized test, which can be administered as early as the sixth grade."

As part of the Service Learning outcomes, the findings and discoveries are presented to CSUMB administrators, faculty, staff, and students&#8212in addition to the rest of CSUMB's neighboring community&#8212in a public presentation. After synthesizing their data and oral histories, the students from the HCOM Service Learning course develop a Recommendations/Action Plan for CSUMB to encourage first generation college students to pursue higher education; the Action Plan is presented at the class public presentation.

The website also includes the findings of three CSUMB representatives from a student panel presentation at the October 2000 Oral History Association Annual Meeting in Durham, North Carolina. The three student presenters from CSUMB, Juanita López, Jaimie Dunham, and Jacinto Salazar assisted in creating a separate section of the website that features their findings.

NOTE: An up-to-date version of the First Generation College Students website can be found at: http://hcom.csumb.edu/courses/HCOM350-02/.

* The FOCUS Summer Bridge program is designed to reach students&#8212usually from historically and currently marginalized and underrepresented groups&#8212who might not have considered college an option, but now with the support of a multifaceted program will have a greater chance to succeed academically.
