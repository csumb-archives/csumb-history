In the summer of 2000, Governor Gray Davis signs the 2000-2001 appropriations bill, which contained the State of California's first capital outlay expenditure for construction of CSUMB's new Science/Academic Center. The facility, which will be the six-year-old campus' first new building, will provide state-of-the-art facilities for innovative science education.

The State appropriation of $14,450,000 will represent the core of a public-private partnership to construct the 64,000-square-foot Center. A community Facilities Committee, chaired by Bob Antle, is working to raise the private and corporate gifts that, along with State Capital Outlay funds, will support the development of the $22.1 million complex.

"We are extremely grateful for the support of Senator McPherson and Assemblyman Keeley for their guidance and support for this project," says CSUMB President, Peter Smith. "This was the first time the State of California appropriated capital outlay funds to build a brand new building on this campus."

To date, CSUMB has received more than $60 million in federal grants to renovate, reconstruct and convert to academic purposes facilities originally built by the Army.
