--- layout: history title: Compaq Makes Contribution to Math HUGE ---

Compaq Computers makes a contribution of more than $14,000 worth of computer hardware to the Math Huge Faculty. Math Huge is an experiment in delivering both remedial math and regular math courses in an innovative, collaborative, and technologically-enriched format.

The pioneering group of faculty members from the Institute for Mathematical Sciences and Applications has developed a prototype of a 60-seat, 30-computer-terminal mathematics learning lab designed to harness the power of collaborative student learning. In the current model, students collaborate in teams of three, using cooperative learning techniques and problem-solving methods to achieve success in their math studies. The lab is designed to expand to 90 seats.

Faculty members teaching in Math Huge, along with a group of faculty involved in an applied research program coordinated by the Faculty Center, are exploring the efficacy of the model with an eye to its possible replication in other learning settings. In the fall, more than 450 students study in the Math Huge facility.
