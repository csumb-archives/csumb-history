--- layout: history title: Reciprocal University for the Arts Project Helps Watsonville School ---

In spring, at-risk students at Watsonville Community School (WCS)--an alternative education program&#8212create their own masterpieces; in a corridor that has no windows, these students conceive their own vista of the world through a project called "Looking in, Looking Out." It's a collaborative venture between WCS, CSUMB's Institute for Visual and Public Art Large Scale Digital Public Art class, and the Reciprocal University for the Arts Project (RUAP).

The opening on May 9, at Watsonville Community School, showcases the project, which consists of a digital poster series created by students. Students were given cameras to photograph themselves and each other, and then synthesized these photos with words and thoughts from pieces that they wrote about their lives&#8212and youth culture in general. Used as a metaphor to describe emotions and ideas, the images revolve around the theme of weather.

In 1999, CSUMB was one of only six sites selected nationally by the Lila Wallace-Reader's Digest Fund to receive a grant to fund an arts partnership with the surrounding communities.

The Reciprocal University for the Arts (RUAP) is a shared project of CSUMB's Visual and Public Arts Institute (VPA) and the Music and Performing Arts Institute (MPA). RUAP supports the development of relationships between the University and four local communities: Watsonville, Salinas, Seaside, and Monterey. These relationships are embedded within CSUMB's interdisciplinary and service-based arts curriculum. RUAP enables students, faculty, and guest artists to produce performances, projects, and classes in the community and bring community artists, youth supporters, and youth onto campus to teach CSUMB students about their experiences.
