--- layout: history title: Top Seniors Awarded ---

On May 25, the top awards for graduating seniors are presented by CSUMB President Peter Smith in the University Center Ballroom at Senior Awards Night. One student, Merlyn Calderon, becomes the first CSUMB student to win two awards.

The Outstanding Senior awards are given out based on an application process and committee decision. Students can apply to or be nominated for the specific award. Each application contains different criteria for winning the award. The winners are:

President's Award for Exemplary Student Achievement: Nile Duppstadt II

Duppstadt, a 22-year-old Institute for Management and International Entrepreneurship (IMIE&#8212known on most campuses as Business) major from Burlington, Vermont, maintained an overall cumulative grade point average of 3.74 while doing pioneer work as an English as a Second Language tutor; was co-captain of the inaugural soccer team; was a Student Voice Senator (student government); and was member of the first Strategic Planning Committee. The winner of this award is the student speaker at commencement.

Provost's Award for Exemplary Academic Achievement: Sabrina Graziano

Graziano, a student from Rome, Italy who has lived in the Monterey area for seven years, has an impressive list of accomplishments. She successfully balanced a remarkable academic load while facing the challenges of managing multiple roles as student, mother, and immigrant with English as a second language. She transferred to CSUMB from Monterey Peninsula College in the fall of 1998 and completed all her University Learning Requirements and Major Learning Outcomes in just five semesters, finishing with the highest grade point average in Institute for Management and International Entrepreneurship (IMIE).

Outstanding Senior Award for Tri-County Achievement: Merlyn Calderon

Merlyn Calderon, a Global Studies major from Salinas, has an extraordinary record of community involvement and achievement, including community volunteer service with Natividad Medical Center, Planned Parenthood Mar Monte, Monterey County AIDS Project, and various other community agencies and institutions. She is the co-founder of "Mothers of Chualar," an organization which brings about positive change and community empowerment. Calderon has also served as a positive role model on campus through her involvement with Student Voice, Educational Talent Search, and other educational outreach activities.

"I am fighting a battle," Calderon explains. "The battle of changing minds and myths behind college in underrepresented communities. I will carry the CSUMB Vision Statement as a Vision of life in whatever I do and wherever I may be."

Outstanding Senior Award for Social Justice: Ethan Brown

Brown, a 21-year-old Integrated Special Studies major of Progressive Political Action from Redlands, California, has demonstrated a commitment to social justice, both in theory and in practice, within the campus community and the local community. He has provided perspective on the issue of multiculturalism, challenged others to embrace the CSUMB Vision, and has persisted in his efforts to demonstrate how multiculturalism at CSUMB must include the recognition of gay, lesbian, bisexual, and transgender people, and those struggling with sexual identity. His role as an advocate for the underrepresented is best exemplified by his involvement as the Multicultural Senator for Student Voice, where he worked to promote numerous issues affecting women, the disabled, CSUMB employees, ethnic diversity, and the "No on 22" Campaign, which sought to deny domestic partnership rights to same-sex couples. In addition, Ethan has provided leadership on several campus committees and task forces, always promoting a multicultural perspective.

"CSU Monterey Bay gave me the opportunity to grow as a leader and to flourish as an activist," Brown explains. "After graduation, I plan to continue my work towards social justice, both in my personal and professional life. I am honored to be recognized for my Queer Activism on campus and in the community."
