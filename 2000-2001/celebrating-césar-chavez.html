--- layout: history title: Celebrating César Chavez ---

It's the inaugural year of California's César Chavez State Holiday, thanks to Governor Davis, who signed into law Senate Bill 984, creating the César Chavez Day of Service and Learning on March 31.

In 2001, Californians across the state begin what is likely to be an annual tradition of uniting in meaningful service-learning projects that commemorate César Chavez, an American labor and civil rights leader who fought injustice and created a coalition for civil rights that was unprecedented in California's history. Through collaborative efforts of community leaders, groups, and CSUMB, thousands of local Californians&#8212especially K-12 students, college students, families, and community members&#8212engage in service and learning activities that celebrate values of service, sacrifice, tolerance, determination and non-violence that guided the way in which César Chavez lived his life.

The local Calendar of Events include:

  • Kick-Off Press Conference on March 28 at the Steinbeck Center in Salinas ~ Community leaders, including representatives from the community, César E. Chavez Celebration Committee, and representatives from CSUMB are on hand to share about this year's collaboration and initiatives focusing on celebrating and honoring César E. Chavez through service and learning.
  • César E. Chavez Symposium on March 30 at CSUMB's World Theatre ~ This free event provides the opportunity for community members, including K-12 and college students, to learn more about the life and legacy of César Chavez. Activities include: guest speakers who worked with César Chavez, showing of prizewinning videos on César's life and accomplishments, live entertainment including performances by El Teatro Campesino, an art display, lively discussions, and free burrito lunch.
  • Youth & Family César Chavez Days of Service & Learning Kick-off Celebration on March 31 at César Chavez Park, Alisal Community of Salinas ~ Celebration of César Chavez's Birthday with music, food, art exhibit, ballet folkloric groups, poetry readings, basketball tournament for middle & high school students, service projects (includes planting over 100 native trees to establish the César E. Chavez Memorial Forest), community resource fair, and other fun activities for families and children.
  • Awards Ceremony & Dinner/Dance on Saturday, March 31, Salon Guadalupano, Iglecia Cristo Rey (Christ The King Catholic Church), Salinas ~ Awards presented include the César E. Chavez Legacy Award in the following categories: Individual (Man & Woman), Organization, Agency, Educator (Man & Woman). Profits are contributed to the César E. Chavez Scholarship Fund. The Buffet dinner is provided by D'La Colmena, and dance music is courtesy of Broken English.
