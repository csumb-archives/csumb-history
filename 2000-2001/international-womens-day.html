--- layout: history title: International Women's Day ---

Dr. June Jordan is the keynote speaker at the annual celebration of International Women's Day on March 8 at the University Center Ballroom.

CSUMB's Multicultural Feminist Group spearheads the event, which includes performances of Japanese Taiko drumming, CSUMB student singer Charmaigne Scott, a lecture by CSUMB Social and Behavioral Studies faculty Angie Tran, and the keynote speech from UC Berkeley faculty member Dr. June Jordan&#8212the most published African American scholar in the U.S.

The event's theme, "Where is the Love? Nurturing our Activism" is based on an article written by Dr. Jordan. Dr. Tran's speech touches on her studies with women sweatshop workers in her home country of Viet Nam.

NOTE: International Women's Day is recognized as an important day for global connection of women and men of all continents. The very first Women's Day occurred in the U.S. at the turn of the century, in a fast-growing industrialized nation and at the dawn of awareness of women's rights. As part of the peace movement before the impending World War in 1913, Russian women held rallies of protest on the last Sunday of February. Elsewhere in Europe, on or around March 8 of the following year, women held rallies either to protest the war or to express solidarity with their sisters. In 1917, with two million Russian soldiers dead in the war, Russian women again chose the last Sunday in February to strike for "bread and peace." Four days later the Czar was forced to abdicate and the provisional government granted Russian women the right to vote. That historic Sunday fell on March 8 on the Gregorian calendar. (The date was February 23 on the Julian calendar used in Russia.)
