Community radio station KAZU-FM and the Foundation of CSUMB complete an agreement over the summer to transfer KAZU's assets and broadcast license to the university foundation.

"We're really pleased . . ." says Ken Peterson, board president of KAZU, the 23-year-old community radio station. "We believe this is the best way to secure a future for KAZU as an independent community radio voice for the Monterey Bay region, and to preserve the high-quality, diverse programming that has been our hallmark since the beginning."

Discussions between KAZU and the CSUMB Foundation begin in January, when the KAZU board determines that it is in the station's long-term interests to seek a permanent reorganized structure. The CSUMB Foundation agrees to invest up to $150,000 to acquire the station, and states its intention to provide future operational support.

KAZU broadcasts at 90.3 FM, serving communities in Monterey and Santa Cruz counties, and portions of San Benito and Santa Clara counties, with a blend of music and public affairs programming. University president and Foundation board President Peter Smith emphasizes at several public forums that KAZU will remain a community broadcasting voice, in keeping with the university's mission to be the community-based campus for the region. He also states that there are no immediate plans for significant programming changes.
