--- layout: history title: S.E.A. Lab Monterey Launches Maiden Voyage ---

In summer 2000, twenty-eight soon-to-be 7th-graders enjoy a newly developed coastal and ocean science education camp called S.E.A. Lab Monterey Bay.

S.E.A. Lab, a community collaboration and public/private partnership, is created to excite young people about science and the ocean through an intensive residential learning experience that emphasizes fun, hands-on, field-oriented activities. It is also designed to serve as a teaching laboratory for student teachers and experienced educators.

A one-week pilot program is held from July 24-28, taking the students on a journey of guided explorations through the unique habitats of the Monterey Bay National Marine Sanctuary&#8212from the watersheds and rivers, through the coastal environments, and out to the deep sea. Each day they have an opportunity to interact with and work alongside coastal naturalists, marine biologists, and oceanographers, exploring habitats on foot, in kayaks and by pontoon boat and catamaran. They also snorkel, learne the mechanics of remotely operated submarines (ROVs), and experience close encounters with dolphins. And they go home with a certificate of accomplishment handed to them by Congressman Sam Farr.

When not in the field, students stay in Residence Hall 201 on campus. There they are supervised by a group of volunteer counselors that include both CSUMB students and volunteer instructors from area institutions like the Monterey Bay National Marine Sanctuary and U.C. Santa Cruz. The long-term vision is to develop a year-round camp that could welcome 10,000-15,000 elementary- and secondary-school-aged children annually.
