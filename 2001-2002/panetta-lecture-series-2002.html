--- layout: history title: Panetta Lecture Series 2002 ---

Seven months after September 11 and a month after the U.S. invasion of Afghanistan, the 2002 Leon Panetta Lecture Series, Terrorism - Challenges and Impact on American Life, draws together a number of high-profile speakers to address key issues in the post-September 11 world. The series addresses such issues as: "Fighting Terrorism: Diplomacy, Defense and Deficits"; "Governing in Crisis: The Role of War, Money and Politics"; "Covering the War: The Search for Truth and the Threat to Journalists"; and "Tracking the Terrorists: Balancing our Nation's Security and Freedoms." The series is presented by the Leon & Sylvia Panetta Institute for Public Policy.

On April 1, New York Times columnist William Safire speaks with Warren Rudman, former U.S. Senator for New Hampshire, on the topic of fighting terrorism.

In the second lecture, Norman Ornstein, political analyst at the American Enterprise Institute, speaks on April 22 with Cokie Roberts, Chief Congressional Analyst for ABC, about the complexities of governing in a time of crisis.

On May 13, CBS Special Correspondent Gloria Borger appears with Bernard Shaw, former CNN anchor, to discuss the complexities journalists face in covering the war.

In the final lecture of the series, on June 17, Former FBI director William Sessions, former U.S. Representative Lee Hamilton, and General Barry McGaffrey (USA RET), former Commander in Chief of the U.S. Southern Command, discuss the difficult balance between the nation's security and freedoms in the hunt for terrorists.
