      • State of the University

Beginning its seventh year with enrolled students, CSUMB again enjoys its highest enrollment ever. Approximately 700 freshmen and 450 transfers join roughly 1800 returning students for a total enrollment of more than 3,000 students. Freshman enrollment has increased almost 25 percent from last year and over 50 percent from two years ago. From August 19-21, students move into two new residence halls to accommodate some of the 700 first-time freshmen who will live on campus this year.

This fall, CSUMB welcomes its largest class ever. Enrollment, which was approximately 2,600 full-time-equivalent (FTE) students for the spring 2001 semester, grows to over 3,000 FTE, with the majority of students being first-time freshmen.

"We are expecting a first-year class of over 900 new-continuing [students], and over 450 new freshmen and transfer students," reports Institutional Assessment and Research Director Juan Avalos. (The count is not final until the official census in late September.)
