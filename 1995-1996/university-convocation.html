--- layout: history title: University Convocation ---

According to the dictionary, convocation means "a ceremonial assembly of members of a university."

Just two months into CSUMB's first year, students, faculty, and administrators meet to address problems related to the university's startup. On October 25, classes are cancelled for the day as the campus community meets in the newly-named University Center (formerly the Pomeroy Center). Despite the operational difficulties, students are mostly satisfied with the university's accomplishments to date.

"I love this school," says Kelly Osborne of Chico [not the Kelly Osbourne of MTV fame]. "They're not teaching us from some old book that's ten years old. Some of the issues are annoying, [but] those things are going to be cleared up in time."

Student grievances range from confusion over graduation requirements, overall campus safety-especially adequate lighting around the center of campus-and a lack of nighttime and weekend activities. Despite the hassles with construction of classrooms (and constant renovations to the library and other buildings), many agree with one student's assessment: "Once they iron out the wrinkles, I think it'll work."

Ten years later, one Pioneer remembers the convocation as a turning point in her education: "For most students, it became clear that they [campus personnel] needed the students to give feedback and that it was very much wanted. It was probably the first time we were asked to give input in our lives. I felt very involved after that. And then it was great to see many of the [solutions proposed] come to fruition."
