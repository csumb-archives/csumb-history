--- layout: history title: Outward Bound ---

About 170 students (freshmen and sophomores) and some faculty and administrators, including the Provost, fill four buses that leave campus on a Sunday for a three-day Outward Bound wilderness experience in the Santa Cruz mountain redwoods.

The trust- and team-building exercises are intended both to challenge participants and to invite participation in group dynamics, in both collaborative and leadership roles. The events include the infamous Ropes Course, designed to promote self-confidence and problem-solving skills while requiring communication and participation to complete. In the Day Hike, people split into groups, are given a rough map of how to get back to camp, are dropped off somewhere in the woods and told "you're on your own." (An Outward Bound instructor stays with each group to ensure that no one is hurt and that the groups all get back to camp&#8212eventually.)

Many attendees remember the challenging experiences mirroring their experiences at CSUMB, and that the best part of the Outward Bound adventure was getting to know peers, faculty, and administrators in a nonacademic setting. "We were making core friends," recalls one freshman Pioneer.

"There was a lot of relationship building; we all knew each other's names by the end. Throughout my time at CSUMB, I always knew I could count on the people I met there."
