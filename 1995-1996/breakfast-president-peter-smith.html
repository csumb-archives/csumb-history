--- layout: history title: Breakfast with President Peter Smith ---

Also known as the "President's Breakfast Get-Together," these Tuesday gatherings are open to all members of the campus community. Over coffee and doughnuts, Dr. Smith sits with anyone who needs or wants to talk with him. Students, faculty, staff, and administrators show up to talk about ideas, complaints, anything. It becomes clear to all that this president wants to hear from you.

One student recounts that these regular meetings were "a great way of finding out what other people did on campus, and gave everyone access to the 'higher-ups' in an unusual way. I mean, a campus president who now knows who you are and what program you're in?"

During these breakfasts, Dr. Smith learns about issues and concerns on campus, gets feedback on policies, and hears about needs and ideas directly from community members. People get to know him, and he gets to know the people of the community.

In CSUMB's first semester, these gatherings are student-specific breakfasts on both Tuesdays and Thursdays. In time, they are reduced to Tuesdays only, while "brown bag" lunches (on Thursdays) become opportunities for staff and faculty to meet with the president. For many, it is a startling and breakthrough experience, signaling how all "higher-ups" are expected to work with students and one another.
