--- layout: history title: Student Voice Gets Its Start ---

Despite the university's efforts to start a student government within the classroom milieu, meetings begin among several students in December in order to have an immediate, recognized voice in campus decision-making. Among the students credited with being the founding members of Student Voice are Sara McClellan, Bethtina Woodridge, and Josh Callahan.

"Student government was supposed to emerge out of a freshmen-sophomore ProSeminar class," Greg Balza remembers. "It sort of evolved from that, with the intention of having a new model, an alternative structure. Many of the upper-division students knew there were some serious nuts-and-bolts issues that needed to be addressed that wouldn't be, unless upperclassmen were participating as well.

"We eventually decided, 'Let's just do it, that's what the campus teaches us&#8212let's do things, let's get it done.' They [campus officials] let you do a lot of stuff; we were able to start student government, we were able to be part of these committees, be a part of the ULRs [University Learning Requirements], have a real impact. We did a lot of the groundwork."
