--- layout: history title: Library Opens: "Wired" for Business ---

In January 1995, plans begin for a building to house book collections and to give students access to study space, email, and, of course, an incredible new resource called the internet. The original three library administrators-librarians Janie Silveira (hired in June), Eddy Hogan, and Steven Watkins (both arriving in August), begin working as many as 16 hours a day on all facets of putting a library together. (NOTE: All three original librarians are still at CSUMB ten years later.) By early October-despite the building not yet being completed-they hire four library assistants and six part-time reference librarians.

On October 16, 1995, the Library Learning Complex opens to students who now have access to proliferating online reference resources. The internet is still in its infancy, so CSUMB-with campus-wide computers, online resources, email, and space designated specifically for students' personal web pages-is ahead of the less "wired" campuses around the country. "It was just about everybody's first exposure to the internet," Eddy Hogan explains. "The timing was just right for this kind of experiment."

Soon, the library will have shelving for the ever-growing book collections. The library's official opening celebration occurs February 20, 1996 and features keynote speaker Brian Lamb, founder of C-SPAN, the cable TV network that began broadcasting live coverage of the U.S. House of Representatives in 1979.
