--- layout: history title: National Media Arrives for CSUMB's Opening ---

CSUMB now consists of 200 total staff, faculty, and administrators (116 employees and 35 faculty, nearly three times the number just eight months earlier), and 654 students. People from very diverse backgrounds are thrown together in one of most unique enterprises in higher education. Some people on campus have held high public offices or earned prestigious degrees. Others are first-generation collegians whose parents work in the fields and don't speak English.

The media takes note of it all; crews with cameras from CNN, ABC News, and NBC News arrive, along with reporters from The Washington Post, The Boston Globe, as well as large regional local media outlets like the San Francisco Chronicle and the San Jose Mercury News. ABC's Nightline comes to town unannounced and begins interviewing students, including the first-ever student admitted to CSUMB, Tiana Thayer of Mammoth.

"It was like the whole world was watching," recalls one administrator.

"There was a sort of spectacle to it all," says one transfer student. "[Fort Ord was] an excluded community and it's right in the Monterey Bay. For years, unless you were military, you couldn't get onto Fort Ord, so reporters were curious. There was a lot of curiosity, something like: 'What is this place all about? And there's a campus there? What did they change?' When I first got here, I thought, 'Where are they gonna put [the campus]?'"
