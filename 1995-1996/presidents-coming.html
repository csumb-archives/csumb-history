--- layout: history title: The President's Coming! ---

A transfer student recounts the following: "I remember the Advance Team [Secret Service] descended on campus [before President Bill Clinton's arrival]. It was Labor Day weekend; it was a big thing, an amazing event. Regardless of your politics, as a college student it's a big deal when the president comes to your campus."

One Pioneer remembers: "It was very surreal having so many different people there. The campus was mostly dirt then, and I remember grown men rolling out huge circular racks of grass in the middle of the rain. We didn't really have any buildings, classes or grass, and there in the mud are these grown men putting in grass [seemingly] just for the president-that was the surreal part. It was inspiring because you had the president there, to be able to be a part of that. Back then there was this sense of being secure and safe on campus. You could leave your car unlocked and get the sense of it still being there. Everyone's looking out for everyone else. A teacher would sit down and eat with students that weren't in the same programs. There weren't those normal boundaries. You learned to adapt to change very quickly. You were either open to new things, or you weren't. And if you weren't you didn't stay. I stayed."

NOTE: Students were intrigued by the Secret Service agents, many of whom were friendly and answered questions. To this day, the FBI shows up each year on Career Day to recruit. Said one Pioneer student who works on campus today: "It's great that they have always seen us as a resource."
